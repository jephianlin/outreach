$2E - 3E + 2E = 6 \implies E = 6$
$V=4, E=6, F=4$

1b. $k=4$
$4V = 2E \implies V = \frac{1}{2}E$

$V-E+F = \frac{1}{2}E - E + \frac{2}{3}E = 2$

$3E - 6E + 4E = 12 \implies E = 12$
$V=6, E=12, F=8$

1c. $k=5$
$5V = 2E \implies V = \frac{2}{5}E$

$V-E+F = \frac{2}{5}E - E + \frac{2}{3}E = 2$

$V=12, E=30, F=20$
